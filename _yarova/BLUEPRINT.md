## 0. Clarity Rules — Every Section Must Pass Before Shipping

One failure = rewrite. Do not ship.

---

### Test 1 — The 3-Question Test (5 seconds)

Show the section for 5 seconds.
Reader must answer all three without help.

| Question | Pass | Fail |
|---|---|---|
| What is this? | 1 sentence, no follow-up | Requires explanation |
| Who is it for? | 1 sentence, obvious | Ambiguous |
| What do I do next? | 1 button, no hesitation | Unclear or missing |

When any answer is "I'm not sure": FAIL. Rewrite.
When all three answered: run Test 2.

---

### Test 2 — Line Count Budget

| Section | Max lines per block | Fail trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | 5 lines total | More than 5 |
| Any body section | 3 lines per block | 4 consecutive non-blank lines |
| Single concept | 1 line | 2 ideas on 1 line |
| FAQ answer | 4 lines | More than 4 |

When any block exceeds limit: FAIL. Split or cut.
When all within limits: run Test 3.

---

### Test 3 — Hemingway Grade

Paste section at hemingwayapp.com.

When grade ≤ 5: PASS.
When grade 6–8: simplify where possible, flag.
When grade > 8: FAIL. Rewrite in plain English.

Why grade 5: buyer is stressed, distracted, job-hunting in a second language.

---

### Applying the Tests

Write copy first.

Test 1 fails → concept is wrong. Rethink the section.
Test 2 fails → format is wrong. Split or cut.
Test 3 fails → language is wrong. Simplify.

All three pass: section ships.
One fails: fix that specific problem, rerun only that test.

## 3. Marketing Strategy

### The 4 Psychological Levers — Apply All 4

**Lever 1: Shame Removal**

Say: "Your brain is not broken. The system is."
Why: the buyer blames themselves. Remove the blame first. Trust spikes immediately.

**Lever 2: Loneliness Relief**

Show: a squad of 4, a Team Lead, three syncs per week.
Why: job hunting alone is demoralizing. Community is the cure.

**Lever 3: Fear Elimination**

State explicitly: we handle resume, interviews, background checks, until probation cleared.
Why: the buyer's real fear is "what if I fail in week two of the new job?"
"Until probation cleared" removes that fear completely.

**Lever 4: Exclusivity**

State: 4 seats per batch. Assessment required to join.
Why: gatekeeping makes them want in more, not less.

**Problem:** Trapped. No experience → no job. No job → no experience.

**Agitate:** Certificates do not fix this. Hiring managers see through tutorials.

**Solve:** Join a real squad. Work real tickets. We handle the rest.

### Dopamine Triggers

| Trigger | How to apply |
|---|---|
| Progress | Daily ticket completion = visible daily wins |
| Belonging | Squad + Team Lead + syncs = community |
| Safety | "Until probation" framing on every CTA |
| Proof | Real placements with city and salary on the page |
| Scarcity | 4 seats. Real number. Never fake. |

---

### Copy Rules — Never Break These

✅ One idea per line.
✅ Max 15 words per line.
✅ Plain English. Define jargon inline on first use.
✅ Name their exact pain — not "job seekers struggle," say "you wake up to another rejection email."
✅ Binary only. "When X: Y. When not X: Z." Never "might" or "could."
✅ Brutally honest. No fake guarantees. Honesty = trust.

---

### Never Write These

❌ "Revolutionary" / "Secret" / "Guaranteed job"
❌ "Bootcamp" / "Course" / "Modules" / "Curriculum"
❌ "Typically" / "Usually" / "Might" / "Could"
❌ Walls of text — more than 3 lines without a blank break
❌ Fake urgency — scarcity must always be a real number

## 16. Enrollment Call Script

### Phase 1 — Set the Frame (2 min)

"Thanks for jumping on. Yarova is a real workplace simulation — not a course.
My job today is a quick technical diagnostic to confirm your baseline matches our ticket requirements.
If you are not ready, I will tell you honestly and give you prep resources.
If you are ready, we will look at open seats for the next batch.
Sound fair?"

---

### Phase 2 — Discovery (3 min)

1. "What is your current visa or work status, and which city are you targeting?"
2. "How many applications in the last 60 days? What response are you getting?"
3. "Where does the interview break down — HR screening or the technical deep-dive?"

---

### Phase 3 — Technical Baseline (7 min)

Do not ask for live code. Assess reasoning only.

**Q1 — Linux:**
"A developer says their script won't run on a fresh Linux instance. What is the first file system check you run, and how do you make it executable?"
Pass: mentions `ls -l`, `chmod +x`, file ownership (`chown`).

**Q2 — Git:**
"You have been on a feature branch for two days. A teammate just merged 5 commits to main. How do you bring your branch up to date cleanly before opening a PR?"
Pass: mentions `git fetch` + `git rebase main` or `git pull --rebase`.

**Q3 — Systems/HTTP:**
"A user gets a 502 Bad Gateway error. Does that mean the server is off? Where do you look first?"
Pass: knows the server is alive but the upstream process is dead. Looks at app logs or Nginx config.

---

### Phase 4 — Score the Candidate

| Signal | Accept | Reject |
|---|---|---|
| Technical baseline | Understands Linux, Git, HTTP errors | Has never touched a terminal |
| Time commitment | 3–4 hours daily + 3 syncs/week | Wants fully self-paced |
| Communication | Articulates a problem clearly | Cannot describe a problem without prompting |

---

### Phase 5 — Close (5 min)

When candidate passes:

"Your baseline is where it needs to be.
Squad slots are capped at 4 so the Team Lead can personally review every PR.
For [Track] starting [Date], I have [N] seats left.
The cost is $2,400 CAD flat. No income share, no hidden fees.
Finish your tickets and don't get placed: you join the next batch free.

Do you want to lock in this batch, or look at dates in [next month]?"

---
